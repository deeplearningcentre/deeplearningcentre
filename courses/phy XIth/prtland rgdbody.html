Lecture Plan: Motion of System of Particles and Rigid Body

Course Overview:

This course introduces the fundamental concepts and principles related to the motion of a system of particles and rigid bodies. We will explore the mechanics of particles and rigid bodies, analyzing the forces, torques, and moments involved, and the resulting motion that follows Newton's Laws.

Lecture Breakdown:

  1. Introduction to System of Particles

    • Definition of a System of Particles
    • Center of Mass
    • Motion of the Center of Mass
    • Reference Frames and Relative Motion

  2. Forces in a System of Particles

    • Internal and External Forces
    • Equation of Motion for a System of Particles
    • Conservation of Momentum

  3. Rotation of Rigid Bodies

    • Definition of Rigid Body
    • Angular Displacement, Velocity, and Acceleration
    • Moment of Inertia
    • Torque and Rotational Analog of Newton's Second Law

  4. Work and Energy in Rotational Motion

    • Work Done by a Rotational Force
    • Rotational Kinetic Energy
    • Conservation of Energy in Rotational Motion

  5. Dynamics of Rigid Bodies

    • Equations of Motion for Rotating Bodies
    • Rolling Motion: Translation and Rotation Combined
    • Moment of Inertia for Different Geometries

  6. Angular Momentum and Its Conservation

    • Angular Momentum of a Point Particle
    • Angular Momentum of a Rigid Body
    • Conservation of Angular Momentum

  7. Collisions of Rigid Bodies

    • Elastic and Inelastic Collisions
    • Impulse and Change in Angular Momentum

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the concepts of the center of mass and its role in particle motion.
  • Learn about the dynamics of a system of particles and rigid bodies under different force conditions.
  • Apply Newton's laws of motion in both linear and rotational contexts.
  • Understand the principle of conservation of momentum and energy in mechanical systems.
  • Analyze and solve problems involving the motion of rigid bodies and their interactions.

Assessment & Evaluation:

Assessment will be based on:

  • Regular quizzes after every key topic
  • Assignments on rotational dynamics and particle motion problems
  • Midterm and final exams
  • Project on real-world applications of system of particles and rigid body motion
