SSL/TLS Lecture Plan by Deep Learning Centre Gurgaon

Introduction

Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) and Transport Layer Security (TLS) are cryptographic protocols designed to provide secure communication over a computer network. This lecture series will cover the fundamentals of SSL/TLS, how they work, and their importance in securing web traffic, especially in HTTPS and other network communications.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the core concepts of SSL and TLS protocols.
  • Distinguish between SSL and TLS and understand the evolution of these protocols.
  • Learn about encryption, authentication, and integrity mechanisms in SSL/TLS.
  • Understand the SSL/TLS handshake process and its security features.
  • Explore real-world applications of SSL/TLS (e.g., HTTPS, email security).

Lecture Topics

  • Week 1: Introduction to SSL/TLS and its importance in web security
  • Week 2: The history and evolution of SSL to TLS
  • Week 3: SSL/TLS Protocol Structure and Components
  • Week 4: SSL/TLS Handshake: Process and Phases
  • Week 5: Cryptography in SSL/TLS: Symmetric and Asymmetric Encryption
  • Week 6: SSL/TLS Certificates: Structure, Types, and Validation
  • Week 7: Implementing SSL/TLS in Web Servers and Clients
  • Week 8: Security Considerations and Attacks on SSL/TLS (e.g., Man-in-the-Middle, Heartbleed)
  • Week 9: Troubleshooting SSL/TLS Issues
  • Week 10: SSL/TLS Best Practices and Future Trends

Lecture Timeline
